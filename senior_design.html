Senior Design - Project ALIS

Aerial Land Inspection System

For our Senior design project, we are working with Vermeer to map a geographical area autonomously using a quadcopter.

Our job is to improve the current system, which is able to control the flight of the quadcopter and take pictures. The end goal is for the program to take in two delimiting coordinates and send the quadcopter over the designated area and come back to us with a map of the area.

My official is Webmaster, however this will not be the bulk of my contribution. I will mainly be participating in working on the Java codebase. My goal is also to get OpenLayers onto our project website, this is technology that will enable us to upload and visualize the maps the quadcopter creates.

The purpose of the product is so that Vermeer can go into sparsely mapped areas, such as can be found in Africa, and generate high definition maps. These maps will help them analyse an area and coordinate with local authorities, so that they do not end up drilling in areas where they shouldn't. This is important, because there are times when lack of such knowledge can cause operators to hit pipelines or cables.
